Skyline Wayanad

Chembra Peak

14 km from Kalpetta
Recommended: 4-5 Hours

The Heart of the Valley

Chembra Peak is the highest point in Wayanad, standing tall at 2,100 meters above sea level. It is globally recognized for the heart-shaped crystal-clear lake, 'Hridaya Saras', located mid-way to the peak, which is believed to have never dried up.

Trekking to Chembra is a moderately challenging but highly rewarding experience. As you ascend through the rolling misty grasslands and rugged terrain, the entire expanse of Wayanad opens up below you. On a clear day, the views can reach as far as the Kozhikode and Malappuram districts.

Travel Tip: Trekking permits are limited to 200 people per day. We strongly suggest arriving at the forest office at Meppadi by 7:00 AM to secure yours. Trekking up to the very peak is currently restricted due to conservation; the endpoint is usually the heart lake.

Trekker's Paradise

The trek involves a steady climb through beautiful tea gardens and mountain slopes. A forest-approved guide is mandatory and will be provided by the forest department at the base of the trek. The route is physically demanding but offers some of the most dramatic landscapes in all of Kerala.

Visitor Essentials

  • Timings: 7:00 AM - 12:00 PM (Permit issue start).
  • Trekking Fee: Approx. ₹750 for a group of 10.
  • Provisions: Wear sturdy trekking boots. Carry water.
  • Best Season: September to March.

Trek Logistics

Early starts are non-negotiable for Chembra. We can arrange your early morning transport to Meppadi and even pack a trekking lunch for you.
